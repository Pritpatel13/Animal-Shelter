Hello, I am an Iguana.

I come from South America, where I spend my days basking in warm sunlight, eating fresh leaves, fruits, and flowers. I love climbing tree branches and exploring the forests that have been my home for generations.

The forest is where I belong. It is where I feel safe, free, and alive.

But my peaceful life was taken away by smugglers.

They captured me from my natural home and sold me for a high price simply because I look unique and exotic. To them, my unusual appearance is a way to make money.

But just because I look different does not mean I belong in a cage.

I am not a decoration. I am not a collectible. I am a living creature with the same right to live freely as any other being.

After being captured, many of us are kept in cramped containers with little space to move and very little fresh air. Some of my fellow iguanas do not survive the journey. They suffer from stress, dehydration, and suffocation.

Imagine being taken away from everything you know... your home, your family, your freedom... all because someone wanted to profit from you.

That is the reality many of us face.

Thankfully, kind-hearted rescue teams stepped in to save us.

They gave us safety, proper care, fresh food, and the space we desperately needed. Most importantly, they treated us with kindness and respect.

Because of them, I have a second chance at life.

They showed me that while some humans cause harm, others dedicate their lives to protecting animals like me.

So let me ask you something...

How would you feel if someone took you away from your home, locked you in a cage, and decided your entire life was worth nothing more than a price tag?
