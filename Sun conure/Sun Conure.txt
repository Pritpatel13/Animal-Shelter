Hello, I am a Sun Conure.

I come from the beautiful forests of South America. My days are meant to be filled with sunshine, fresh fruits, flowers, and seeds. I fly through the trees, play with my flock, and enjoy the freedom of the wild.

People are often fascinated by my bright colors and beautiful feathers. They admire how cheerful and playful I am.

But many people only see my beauty... they do not see my need for freedom.

Because of my colorful feathers and friendly nature, I am often trapped and sold illegally in markets. Smugglers take me away from my home, my family, and everything I have ever known, simply to make money.

Imagine waking up one day and finding yourself locked inside a cage, unable to fly, unable to explore, and unable to return home.

The loneliness is overwhelming. The stress is constant. Many of us spend our days frightened and confused.

Some birds are even killed for their feathers, which are used as decorations. Others are kept in tiny cages where they receive little care, little attention, and very little space to move.

We may not speak your language, but we feel pain. We feel fear. We feel loneliness.

Some trapped birds become so stressed that they pull out their own feathers. Others scream endlessly, hoping someone will hear their suffering.

Yet smugglers rarely care. Their only goal is profit, no matter how much suffering they cause.

Thankfully, there are people who do care.

Team Prayas rescued me and many others like me. They understood our pain when others ignored it.

They gave us clean water, healthy food, safe shelter, and enough space to move freely. Most importantly, they treated us with kindness and compassion.

Because of them, I have a chance to live the life I deserve.

Thank you, Team Prayas, for protecting us and giving us hope.

Before I go, let me ask you one question...

If someone locked you inside a room for the rest of your life, took away your freedom, and denied you the space you needed to live... would you be comfortable?

Then why should any bird be?
