Hello, I am an African Grey Parrot.

I come from the lush forests of West Africa. My home is among the tall trees, not behind the bars of a cage. I live with my family, and every day we fly together in search of fruits, nuts, and seeds.

Many people know me for my intelligence. I can imitate sounds and even copy human words. People are often amazed by my ability to communicate.

But what makes me special has also put me in danger.

Because people want to own me, smugglers steal us from our homes. Baby parrots are taken from their nests before they even have a chance to grow up. Others are trapped in nets, stuffed into boxes, or packed into bags.

During these journeys, many of us suffer terribly. We are left without enough food, water, or fresh air. Some never survive the trip.

Imagine being torn away from your family, trapped in a dark box, and carried far away from everything you have ever known.

To smugglers, we are not living creatures. We are merchandise. They sell us for huge amounts of money while ignoring our fear, our suffering, and our right to live freely.

Our ability to mimic human speech should not be a reason to take away our freedom.

We belong in the forests, flying beneath the open sky, not spending our lives locked inside cages.

Thankfully, my story did not end there.

Team Prayas rescued me and many other parrots from this cruel trade. They gave us safety when we were frightened and care when we were suffering.

They provide us with nutritious food, clean water, proper medical treatment, and a safe environment where we can recover and live without fear.

Most importantly, they gave us a second chance at life.

Their kindness reminds us that not all humans are driven by greed. Some choose compassion.

Before I go, let me ask you something...

If someone took you away from your family, locked you in a cage, and decided that your freedom was worth less than money... how would you feel?

Then why should any parrot have to live that way?
